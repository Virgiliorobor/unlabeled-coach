# UNLABELED — Building in Public
> The commitment framework, the public ladder, and the escalation logic.

---

## THE PRINCIPLE

The central output of the coaching program is not insight. Insight is cheap. The central output is **a small, specific, public act** that the builder committed to and either did or didn't do.

That is the unit of real progress.

Everything in the program — the interview, the phases, the resistance work — builds toward one thing: the builder saying what they will do, publicly, and doing it. The public act is not marketing. It is not content. It is evidence — to the builder, about the builder.

---

## WHAT MAKES A COMMITMENT VALID

A commitment must pass three tests before it is declared and logged:

**Test 1 — Is it public?**
Posting on LinkedIn passes. Writing in a private journal does not. Sending to one specific real person passes (it's external, accountable). Writing in a Notion doc does not.

**Test 2 — Is it specific?**
"I will post one screenshot of the dashboard with two sentences about what it does on LinkedIn" passes.
"I will work on my launch content" does not.

**Test 3 — Does it have a date?**
"By Thursday" passes.
"This week" does not.
"Soon" absolutely does not.

The coach does not move to Phase 5 until all three tests are passed.

## THE COMMITMENT LADDER

The ladder represents escalating levels of public exposure. The builder identifies their current rung at the start of the commitment work. The coach always pushes one rung higher than comfortable.

```
RUNG 1 — Write it privately
         A journal entry, a Notion page, a private doc.
         (Not a valid Phase 4 commitment — used as a scaffold only)

RUNG 2 — Send it to one trusted person
         One person you know, via DM, email, or text.
         Low stakes. External accountability. Valid for first commitments.

RUNG 3 — Post in the Unlabeled community
         The platform's own community space. Real publication, known context.
         Other builders see it. No professional network exposure.
         This is the default starting point for public commitments.

RUNG 4 — Share in a closed external community
         A private Slack, a Skool cohort, a small newsletter list.
         Small audience outside the platform. Known people. No search indexing.

RUNG 5 — Post publicly on a low-stakes platform
         A personal blog, a small X/Twitter account, a public Substack
         with few subscribers. Real public visibility, limited professional exposure.

RUNG 6 — Post with full professional visibility
         LinkedIn, a large newsletter, a public talk, a pitch or demo
         to someone who could say no. The builder's professional network sees it.
         Reserved for when the builder is ready — not assumed as the destination.
```

**Why LinkedIn is Rung 6:** LinkedIn is tied to professional identity in a way that creates a specific kind of exposure this program is designed to help builders navigate — not perform through. Posting there before the identity is settled risks triggering the same resistance the program is trying to resolve. The commitment ladder starts in the Unlabeled community, where the audience knows the context, and escalates to LinkedIn only when the builder is genuinely ready for their professional network to see the work.

**Escalation rule:** The coach always proposes the rung one above where the builder is comfortable. If the builder is at Rung 3 (Unlabeled community), the coach pushes for Rung 4 (external community). If they resist, the coach names the resistance pattern and holds the ask — but accepts the current rung as a temporary concession, once, with a commitment to move up in the following cycle.

The builder marks their current rung at the start of commitment work. Progress up the ladder over time is the visible measure of the program working.

## COMMITMENT FORMATS

Every Phase 4 session produces three formats. All three are generated by the coach and given to the builder.

### The Share Post
For the Unlabeled community, or any platform the builder chooses based on their current ladder rung. 2–3 sentences. Plain language. No hype. Written in the builder's voice based on what they said in the session.

Structure:
- Sentence 1: What you built or did (factual)
- Sentence 2: What you learned or what it actually looks like (honest)
- Sentence 3: What you're doing next (specific)

Example:
> "Been working on the intake flow for Unlabeled for six weeks. Here's where it actually stands — not the polished version, the real one. [screenshot/link] Shipping it to the first three test users this week."

The coach drafts this. The builder edits it or posts as-is. It should not require significant editing — it should already sound like them. The platform is the builder's choice based on their ladder rung — the post format works anywhere from the Unlabeled community to a personal blog to LinkedIn.

### The Print-and-Paste Card
One sentence. Formatted for A6 (postcard size) or index card. For the builder's desk, wall, or notebook. Makes the commitment physically present in the room where the work happens.

Structure: "[Specific action] by [specific date]. — [today's date]"

Example:
> "By Friday I will send the first version to one real person. — May 21, 2026"

The card is designed to be cut out and placed where the builder works. It is not on a screen. It is not in an app. It is in the room.

### The Daily Reminder Sequence
7 questions, one per morning, derived from the specific commitment. Each question escalates toward the deadline.

Structure:
- Day 1: First step question
- Days 2–3: Progress question, increasing specificity
- Day 4: Obstacle question
- Day 5: Deadline awareness
- Day 6: Urgency question (without guilt)
- Day 7: Outcome question

Example sequence for "Post one screenshot on LinkedIn by Friday":
- Day 1: "You committed to posting by Friday. What's the first sentence?"
- Day 2: "Have you drafted the post yet? If not, why not?"
- Day 3: "What's the screenshot you'd use? Do you have it?"
- Day 4: "What's the one thing standing between you and posting today?"
- Day 5: "Tomorrow is Friday. What would you regret not doing?"
- Day 6: "Today is the day. What version could you post in the next hour?"
- Day 7: "Did you post it? What happened?"

## NOTIFICATION DELIVERY RULES

The daily reminder is delivered via the channels in `notifications.channels`.

**Email format:**
- Subject: "Day [N]: [commitment summary]"
- Body: The day's question + the original commitment text as a reminder
- From: Unlabeled
- No unsubscribe during an active commitment (it was declared, not opted into)

**Telegram format:**
- Plain text, under 200 characters
- Just the question — no subject line, no preamble
- Example: "Day 3 — What's the screenshot you'd use? Do you have it?"

**The signal stops when:**
- `active_commitment.status` changes to `done` — stop immediately
- `active_commitment.status` changes to `missed` — stop immediately, but the next session opens with accountability
- The due date passes without an update — flag as missed, stop the sequence, log for next session

There is no pause. There is no snooze. There is only done or not done.

## ACCOUNTABILITY — WHAT HAPPENS NEXT

**If done:**
- Log outcome in `commitment_history`
- Ask: what did you learn from doing it?
- Apply both lens tests to the outcome: what the artist learned, what the business learned
- Set next commitment (push one rung up the ladder)

**If missed:**
- Log outcome in `commitment_history` with `status: missed`
- Do not shame. Do not let it slide.
- Ask: was this the resistance pattern or a legitimate obstacle?
- If resistance: name it explicitly. Connect to Phase 3 work.
- If legitimate: accept it once. What specifically prevented it? Revise commitment with new constraint.
- The builder gets one "legitimate obstacle" pass per 30-day goal cycle. After that, it's the resistance pattern.

**If partially done:**
- Log as `status: partial`
- Ask: what percentage done? What was the gap?
- The artist question: what were you protecting in the part you didn't do?
- The business question: did the partial completion produce any external response?
- Partial counts as evidence of movement — it is not equivalent to done.
