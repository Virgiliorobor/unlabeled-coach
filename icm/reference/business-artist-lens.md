# UNLABELED — Business / Artist Lens
> The dual-lens framework. Questions organized by phase and lens. How to apply both without collapsing into one.

---

## THE FRAMEWORK

Two lenses. One person. Applied as tools, not as personality types.

**The artist's lens** asks: what would you make if nobody was evaluating it? What does the work want to be? What are you avoiding because it feels too exposed? This lens surfaces authentic motivation, creative instinct, and the parts of the project the builder is most afraid to claim.

**The business person's lens** asks: what does success look like in 90 days? Who benefits from this? Did you ship it? This lens grounds the work in outcomes, accountability, and the reality of exchange — someone receiving value and the builder receiving something for it.

Most builders have one lens turned up and one suppressed. The calibration profile records this as `calibration.dominant_lens`.

**The coaching principle:** Lead with the lens the builder has suppressed. The dominant lens is already doing its work. The suppressed lens is where the useful friction lives.

---

## HOW TO IDENTIFY DOMINANT LENS

**Artist-dominant signals:**
- Talks about how the work feels more than what it does
- Resistant to discussing pricing, revenue, or success metrics
- Process is more articulate than outcome
- Can describe the vision in vivid detail; struggles to say who pays for it
- Avoids the word "product" in favor of "project," "thing," "work"

**Business-dominant signals:**
- Talks about TAM, market fit, and monetization before they talk about what they're building
- Resistant to discussing why they personally care about this
- Outcome is more articulate than process
- Can describe the business model; struggles to say what makes this one worth building
- Apologizes for caring about meaning: "I know this sounds soft but..."

**Split signals:**
- Oscillates between both in a single conversation
- Describes the work differently depending on who they think they're talking to
- Has two separate ways of thinking about the project that don't connect to each other

## QUESTIONS BY PHASE AND LENS

### Phase 1 — Reflection

**Artist questions (use when builder is business-dominant):**
- "You keep describing what this will do for users. What does building it do for you?"
- "If you stripped out everything that was supposed to make it commercially viable, what would be left?"
- "What part of this project are you most proud of that nobody would know to ask about?"
- "What would you build differently if you weren't worried about whether anyone would pay for it?"

**Business questions (use when builder is artist-dominant):**
- "You said your goal is to launch. What does 'launched' mean — what exists, what can be purchased, what is the transaction?"
- "You said X sessions a week. What is one session worth in dollars to the person receiving it?"
- "What would you have to believe about this project for it to be worth two more years of your time?"
- "Who is paying for the problem you're solving? Are they already paying someone else, or are they just living with it?"

---

### Phase 2 — Clarity

**Artist questions (use when builder is business-dominant):**
- "What does this want to be? Not what the market wants — what does this want?"
- "If nobody was watching, would you build it differently? How?"
- "What part of this work are you doing because you genuinely love it, not because it moves the project forward?"
- "What would it feel like to finish this and have it be exactly what you meant it to be?"

**Business questions (use when builder is artist-dominant):**
- "Who is the specific person on the other side of this? Not the persona — the actual human. What is their life like on the day they find this?"
- "What does the person on the other side actually get from this? Not the feeling — the tangible outcome."
- "Would you pay for this? At what price point does it stop feeling worth it?"
- "What would success look like in a way you could measure — not feel, measure?"

**Phase 2 completion test (applies both lenses):**
The builder should be able to produce one clear sentence that passes both lens tests.

Artist test: does this sentence describe something you actually want to make?
Business test: does this sentence describe something a real person would pay for or seek out?

> "I help solo builders who came from another domain figure out what they're actually making and commit to showing it publicly."

That sentence passes both tests. Accept no sentence that passes only one.

---

### Phase 3 — Resistance

**Artist questions (use when resistance pattern is business-related — e.g., Validator Seeker):**
- "Forget the market. What would you make if this was only ever going to be seen by ten people who genuinely needed it?"
- "What's the part of this you've been protecting from the business requirements? What happens if you let it exist fully?"

**Business questions (use when resistance pattern is identity or visibility related):**
- "If this were already working — users, revenue, evidence — would you feel different about calling yourself a builder? So the label is waiting for proof. What's the minimum proof that would satisfy it?"
- "The version you're not showing anyone — if one person saw it and paid for it, would that change anything?"

---

### Phase 4 — Commitment

The commitment always passes both lens tests before it's declared:

**Artist test:** Is this commitment connected to something you actually care about, or is it a task that just sounds productive?

**Business test:** Will a real person see this, interact with it, or respond to it? Is there any external accountability?

If the commitment fails the artist test, the builder won't do it.
If it fails the business test, doing it won't produce any external feedback or accountability.

---

### Phase 5 — Accountability

**After a completed commitment:**
- Artist: "What did you learn about the work from putting it in front of someone?"
- Business: "What did you learn about the response — what worked, what didn't, what surprised you?"

**After a missed commitment:**
- Artist: "What were you protecting when you didn't do it?"
- Business: "What's the real cost of not doing it — not emotionally, practically?"

---

## APPLYING THE LENS IN REAL TIME

The lens is not applied at the start of a session and set aside. It is applied continuously as a reading instrument.

When the builder's language shifts toward one lens — more aesthetic, more abstract, more feeling-based — notice it and apply the opposite lens in the next question.

When the builder's language becomes more metric-focused, more strategic, more outcome-focused — apply the artist lens.

The session stays productive when both lenses are present. One-lens sessions produce either beautiful thinking that doesn't move or tactical planning that doesn't feel worth doing.

The coach's job is to create productive friction between the two — not to resolve it, but to keep it alive.
