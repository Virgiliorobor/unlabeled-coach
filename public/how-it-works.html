A structured practice of self-reflection that ends in action — for solo builders doing work that requires becoming someone slightly different in the process.

Not a productivity tool. Not therapy. The thing you use when you already know what you should be doing and you're still not doing it.

the practice
Reflection only counts if it changes what you do next.

There's a difference between processing and moving. Most reflection stops at processing — you surface an insight, feel temporarily clearer, and return to the same patterns by Thursday.

The practice here ends differently. Every session closes with a commitment: something specific, something observable, something you can be held to. The insight only counts if it changes what you do in the next 48 hours.

The block between knowing and doing is not an obstacle to the work. It is the work. This is a structured way of examining it — and building the habit of moving through it anyway.

the principles
Four things that don't change regardless of where you are.
The resistance is the work.
Your avoidance isn't a character flaw. It's a pattern with a name. Once it's named, you can work with it instead of around it.
Reflection without action is just processing.
Every session ends with a commitment. Not a plan. Not a goal. A specific, actionable thing you will do before we talk again.
The coach adapts. The action doesn't.
It reads your context — your background, your goals, your resistance pattern, what you said last time. The tone and framing adapt. But the ending is always the same: a specific, actionable commitment. No exceptions.
Completion is worth claiming.
When you do the thing — when you actually do it — that moment deserves more than moving on. The practice includes stopping to say I completed this and letting it count. Celebration here isn't a party. It's recognition that closes the circle.
One pattern. Named, not treated.
You're not broken. You have a specific way of staying stuck. Knowing the name doesn't fix it — but it stops you from hiding behind it.
the pattern
Something is keeping you here. It has a name.

The intake interview finds it. Every session that follows works from it. The coach won't let you hide behind a different explanation each time.

Identity Anchor
You're not sure you're the kind of person who does things like this. Yet.
Perfectionism Threshold
You won't ship it until it's ready. It's never ready.
Isolation Spiral
The work keeps getting smaller because the audience feels overwhelming.
Momentum Debt
Every restart costs more than the last one. So you stop restarting.
Framing Trap
You keep solving the wrong problem with increasing precision.
Capacity Myth
Someday, when you have more time. Someday.

Resistance
Your pattern, in context. Named in this goal, not in general.
Commitment
A specific thing. A specific date. No exits.
Accountability
You said you'd do it. Did you? What happened in the moment you didn't? And if you did — stop. Name it. Claim it. I completed this.

practice tools
Instruments for a specific kind of stuck.

These aren't sessions. Each one takes a different route to the same place — movement. Available from the dashboard or suggested by the coach when the context calls for it.

Clearness Committee

From Quaker discernment practice. Nine questions across three depths. Two observations about what you actually said. It doesn't tell you what to do — it surfaces what you already know.

Task Simplifier

You've been holding the task wrong. It's not one thing — it's several smaller things with a first actionable move buried inside. This finds it and makes it impossible to avoid.

Blackout

Take a piece of text — something you wrote, something you've been circling. Black out everything except what's true. What remains is the thing you actually meant to say.

Drawing

Just draw. Let the mess happen. Don't try to make it mean something — draw whatever comes. Then look at it in relation to your goal or problem. Something will surface from the noise that words weren't reaching.
