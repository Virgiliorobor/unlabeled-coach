A structured practice of self-reflection that ends in action — for solo builders doing work that requires becoming someone slightly different in the process.

the practice
Reflection only counts
if it changes what you do next.
There's a difference between processing and moving. Most reflection stops at processing — you surface an insight, feel temporarily clearer, and return to the same patterns by Thursday.
The practice here ends differently. Every session closes with a commitment: something specific, something observable, something you can be held to. The insight only counts if it changes what you do in the next 48 hours.
The block between knowing and doing is not an obstacle to the work. It is the work. This is a structured way of examining it — and building the habit of moving through it anyway.
the principles
The resistance is the work.
Your avoidance isn't a character flaw. It's a pattern with a name. Once it's named, you can work with it instead of around it.
Reflection without action is just processing.
Every session ends with a commitment. Not a plan. Not a goal. A specific thing you will do before we talk again.
The coach doesn't let you off the hook.
It remembers what you said. It asks about what you didn't do. It's direct in a way that feels uncomfortable and necessary.
One pattern. Named, not treated.
You're not broken. You have a specific way of staying stuck. Knowing the name doesn't fix it — but it stops you from hiding behind it.
the pattern
Something is keeping you here.
It has a name.

The intake interview finds it. The coach works from it.

Identity Anchor You're not sure you're the kind of person who does things like this. Yet.
Perfectionism Threshold You won't ship it until it's ready. It's never ready.
Isolation Spiral The work keeps getting smaller because the audience feels overwhelming.
Momentum Debt Every restart costs more than the last one. So you stop restarting.
Framing Trap You keep solving the wrong problem with increasing precision.
Capacity Myth Someday, when you have more time. Someday.
the session
Each goal moves through six phases. The coach meets you wherever the goal is.

Intake Named and placed. The goal exists now.
Reflection The contradictions surface. Why you want it and why you haven't moved.
Clarity The goal in one sentence. Precise enough to be wrong.
Resistance Your pattern, in context. Named in this goal, not in general.
Commitment A specific thing. A specific date. No exits.
Accountability You said you'd do it. Did you? What happened in the moment you didn't?
